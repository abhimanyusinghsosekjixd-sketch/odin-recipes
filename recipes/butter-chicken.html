Butter Chicken Recipe

Description

Butter Chicken is a classic Indian dish made with marinated & grilled chicken (Tandoori chicken), simmered in a creamy tomato gravy/curry. The sauce is super silky, buttery, aromatic and mildly spicy. This lip-smacking and delicious dish is hugely popular among the Indian food lovers across the world.

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 500g boneless chicken thighs (preferred for juiciness) or breast, cut into 1-inch pieces.
  • Marinade: 1/3 cup Greek yogurt (or hung curd), 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 tbsp Kashmiri red chilli powder (for colour), 1 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp salt, and 1 tbsp lemon juice.
  • Gravy Base: 2-3 tbsp butter, 1 cup tomato puree (passata), 1 tsp sugar or honey (to balance acidity), and 1 tbsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves)—the "secret" ingredient for that restaurant aroma.
  • Finish: 1/3 cup heavy cream and a final knob of butter.

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Combine the chicken with the marinade ingredients in a bowl. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, though 12–24 hours is best for maximum tenderness.
  2. Pan-sear: Heat oil in a skillet and cook chicken in batches until lightly charred and just cooked through.
  3. In the same pan, melt butter and sauté whole spices (cinnamon, cardamoms, cloves) for a minute.
  4. Add tomato puree, salt, and chilli powder. Simmer for 10–15 minutes until it thickens and oil starts to separate.
  5. Pro Tip: For a super-smooth texture, blend the sauce and strain it through a sieve before moving to the next step.
  6. Combine & Simmer: Add the cooked chicken pieces (and any juices) into the sauce. Pour in about 1/2 cup hot water if the gravy is too thick. Simmer for 5–7 minutes.
  7. Final Touch: Stir in the sugar, crushed Kasuri Methi, and heavy cream. Turn off the heat immediately to prevent the cream from splitting. Garnish with fresh coriander.
